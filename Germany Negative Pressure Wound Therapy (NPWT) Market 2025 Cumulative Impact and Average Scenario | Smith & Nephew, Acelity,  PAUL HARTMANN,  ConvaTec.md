Negative-Pressure Wound Therapy (NPWT) is a therapeutic technique (or) sealed wound-care system that involves usage of a vacuum dressing to promote healing of large chronic persistent wounds and acute complicated wounds. NPWT is also used for rapid healing of second- and third-degree burns.  The system consists of an electronically controlled pump and a foam dressing that drains the wound. An adjustable negative pressure is applied via an airtight adhesive film that covers the wound.
